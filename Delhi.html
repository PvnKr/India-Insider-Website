1. Old Delhi.

Old Delhi, also known as Shahjahanabad, is a historic neighborhood located in the northern part of the city of Delhi, India. It was founded by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century and served as the capital of the Mughal Empire until the 19th century. Old Delhi is known for its narrow streets, bustling markets, and ancient monuments such as the Red Fort, Jama Masjid, and Chandni Chowk. The area is also famous for its mouth-watering street food, including parathas, chaat, and jalebis. Despite being a crowded and chaotic area, Old Delhi is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, and food. It provides a glimpse into the rich and diverse past of Delhi and India.




2. Akshardham Temple.

Akshardham Temple, also known as Swaminarayan Akshardham, is a Hindu temple located in Delhi, India. It was inaugurated in 2005 and is one of the largest Hindu temples in the world. The temple complex is spread over 100 acres and includes a main temple, a cultural center, a water show, and a garden. The temple's architecture is a blend of traditional Hindu and modern styles and features intricate carvings and sculptures. Akshardham Temple is not just a place of worship but also a center for art, culture, and education. It attracts millions of visitors every year, who come to admire its beauty and learn about Hinduism and Indian culture. Overall, Akshardham Temple is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in spirituality, architecture, and culture.



3. India Gate.

India Gate is a war memorial located in the heart of Delhi, India. It was built in 1931 in memory of the Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Empire during World War I. The monument is a 42-meter high arch made of sandstone and granite and stands at the center of a large circular area known as the Rajpath. The names of more than 13,000 soldiers are inscribed on the walls of India Gate. India Gate is not just a memorial but also a popular spot for picnics and evening walks. It is beautifully lit up at night and provides a stunning backdrop for photography. Overall, India Gate is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, architecture, and culture. It is a symbol of India's rich past and its sacrifices for the greater good



4. Lotus Temple.

Lotus Temple, also known as the Bahai House of Worship, is a unique and stunning temple located in Delhi, India. It was completed in 1986 and is shaped like a lotus flower, with 27 petals made of white marble. The temple is open to people of all religions and serves as a place of meditation and prayer. The interior of the temple is spacious and serene, with natural light filtering through the petals and creating a peaceful atmosphere. Lotus Temple is not just a place of worship but also a center for art, culture, and education. It attracts millions of visitors every year, who come to admire its beauty and learn about the Bahai faith and its teachings of unity and diversity. Overall, Lotus Temple is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in spirituality, architecture, and culture. It is a symbol of India's secular and inclusive values.



5. Qutub Minar.

Qutub Minar is a historic tower located in Delhi, India. It was built in the 12th century by the Delhi Sultanate ruler Qutb-ud-din Aibak and stands at a height of 73 meters. The tower is made of red sandstone and features intricate carvings and inscriptions. It is surrounded by several other historic monuments, including the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar of Delhi. Qutub Minar is not just a monument but also a popular tourist attraction and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It attracts millions of visitors every year, who come to admire its beauty and learn about India's rich history and culture. Overall, Qutub Minar is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, architecture, and culture. It is a symbol of India's past glory and its remarkable achievements in the field of art and architecture.
