1. Radhanagar Beach .

Radhanagar Beach, also known as Beach No. 7, is one of the most popular and beautiful beaches in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Located on the western coast of Havelock Island, it boasts of crystal clear waters, pristine white sand, and stunning views of the sunset. The beach is a haven for nature lovers and beach enthusiasts alike, offering visitors a chance to relax, unwind and soak in the natural beauty of the surroundings. The beach is also a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports such as snorkeling, scuba diving, and sea walking. Visitors can also take a leisurely walk along the beach, indulge in some beach games, or simply relax and enjoy the stunning views of the turquoise blue sea. Radhanagar Beach is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a serene and peaceful beach getaway and is sure to leave you mesmerized with its natural beauty.


2. Cellular Jail.

Cellular Jail, also known as Kala Pani, is a former colonial prison located in Port Blair, Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Built by the British in 1906, the jail was used to imprison political prisoners who were fighting for India's independence from British rule. The jail is now a national memorial and a popular tourist attraction, offering visitors a glimpse into India's struggle for independence. The architecture of the jail is unique and fascinating, with seven wings radiating out from a central watchtower. The cells were designed in such a way that prisoners were kept in solitary confinement, and communication between them was impossible. Today, the jail houses a museum that showcases the history and struggles of India's freedom fighters. The sound and light show held in the evening is a must-see attraction, as it takes visitors on a journey through the lives of the prisoners and their fight for freedom. Cellular Jail is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in India's history and the struggle for independence.



3.Ross Island.

Ross Island, located in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, is a picturesque island that was once the administrative headquarters of the British Raj. Today, the island is a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a glimpse into the colonial history of the region. The island is surrounded by crystal clear waters and is home to numerous historical buildings, including a church, a bakery, a hospital, and a swimming pool, all of which are in ruins today. Visitors can take a leisurely walk around the island and explore the remains of the buildings, which provide an insight into the colonial architecture of the time. The island is also home to a range of flora and fauna, including deer, peacocks, and various species of birds. A visit to Ross Island is a must for anyone interested in history and nature, and it provides a serene and peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life.




4.mahatma gandhi marine national park.

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is a protected area located in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Established in 1983, the park covers an area of 281.5 square kilometers and is home to a diverse range of marine life. The park is made up of a group of 15 islands, which are surrounded by pristine blue waters, and visitors can enjoy activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, and glass-bottom boat rides to explore the rich underwater world. The park is home to a variety of coral reefs, sea turtles, saltwater crocodiles, and numerous species of fish, making it an ideal destination for wildlife enthusiasts. In addition to the marine life, the park is also home to lush mangrove forests and pristine beaches, which add to the natural beauty of the area. Visitors can also take a walk on the nature trails and observe the different bird species found in the park. A visit to Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is a must for anyone looking to explore the rich biodiversity of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.



5.Elephant Beach.

Elephant Beach is a popular tourist destination located on Havelock Island, in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. The beach is known for its pristine white sand, turquoise waters, and abundant marine life, making it an ideal destination for snorkeling and scuba diving. The coral reefs surrounding the beach are home to a diverse range of marine life, including colorful fish, sea turtles, and even reef sharks. Visitors can take a boat ride to the beach, which is surrounded by lush green forests, and enjoy a day of sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling. The beach is also popular for its water sports activities such as banana boat rides, jet skiing, and parasailing. Elephant Beach offers a serene and peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
