1. Char Dham.

Char Dham Yatra is a pilgrimage tour to the four holy shrines of Hinduism: Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri and Yamunotri. It is believed that visiting these four sacred places washes away sins and helps attain Moksha (salvation). The journey takes place in the hilly state of Uttarakhand in India and attracts a large number of devotees from all over the world. The yatra starts from Yamunotri, followed by Gangotri, Kedarnath, and finally, Badrinath. The scenic beauty and the spiritual ambiance of these places are truly enchanting. The yatra usually takes around 10-12 days to complete, and tourists can experience a unique blend of spirituality and adventure during this time.




2. Mussoorie.

Mussoorie is a beautiful hill station located in the state of Uttarakhand, India. It is popularly known as the "Queen of Hills" and attracts tourists from all over the world. The place is known for its scenic beauty, pleasant climate, and adventure activities like trekking, camping, and paragliding. The main attractions of Mussoorie include Kempty Falls, Gun Hill, Lal Tibba, and the Mussoorie Lake. The place is also famous for its colonial architecture and is home to many old buildings like the Christ Church and the Savoy Hotel. Mussoorie is an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful and rejuvenating break amidst nature.




3. Nainital.

Nainital is a picturesque hill station situated in the northern state of Uttarakhand, India. It is a popular tourist destination and is known for its scenic beauty and serene environment. The main attraction of Nainital is the Naini Lake, which is surrounded by lush green hills and is perfect for boating and other water activities. The place is also famous for its ancient temples like the Naina Devi Temple and the Hanuman Garhi Temple. Other popular tourist spots in Nainital include Tiffin Top, Snow View Point, and the Eco Cave Gardens. Nainital is an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful and relaxing break amidst nature.




4. Rishikesh.

Rishikesh is a popular destination for adventure sports in India. The town is situated on the banks of the Ganges river, and the surrounding hills offer a range of adventure activities. One of the most popular adventure sports in Rishikesh is white water rafting. The river Ganges offers rapids ranging from grade 1 to grade 5, making it a thrilling experience for both beginners and experienced rafters. Another popular adventure activity in Rishikesh is bungee jumping. The jump takes place from a height of 83 meters and offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding hills and the Ganges river. Other adventure sports in Rishikesh include zip-lining, rock climbing, and hiking. The town is also a popular starting point for treks in the Himalayas. Overall, Rishikesh is a great destination for adventure enthusiasts looking to experience the thrill of outdoor sports in a beautiful natural setting.




5. Auli.

Auli is a popular ski destination located in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, India. It is situated at an altitude of 2,500 to 3,050 meters above sea level and offers stunning views of the Himalayan mountain range. Auli is a perfect destination for adventure seekers and skiing enthusiasts. It is surrounded by coniferous and oak forests, making it a picturesque destination for nature lovers. Auli also offers various winter sports activities like skiing, snowboarding, and trekking. It has some of the best ski slopes in the country and is often referred to as the "Skiing Capital of India." Overall, Auli is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an adrenaline-filled adventure in the lap of nature.
