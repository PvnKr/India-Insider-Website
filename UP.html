1. Taj Mahal.

Taj Mahal is a world-renowned white marble mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Taj Mahal is considered one of the greatest examples of Mughal architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is famous for its intricate carvings, beautiful gardens, and stunning reflection in the water. Visitors from all over the world come to see the Taj Mahal, which is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world. The Taj Mahal is a symbol of love, beauty, and the rich cultural heritage of India.




2. Fatehpur Sikri.

Fatehpur Sikri is a historical city located near Agra in Uttar Pradesh, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Akbar in the late 16th century and served as the capital of the Mughal Empire for a brief period. Fatehpur Sikri is famous for its stunning architecture, including the Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and Diwan-i-Khas. It is also known for the Tomb of Salim Chishti, a famous Sufi saint. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts visitors from all over the world who come to marvel at its beauty and learn about its rich history. Fatehpur Sikri is a testament to the Mughal Empire's cultural and architectural achievements and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in India's rich history and heritage.




3. Kashi Vishwanath Temple.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most famous Hindu temples in the world, located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva, and is believed to be one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are considered to be the most sacred Shiva shrines in India. The temple has a rich history and has been destroyed and rebuilt many times over the centuries. It is known for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and religious significance. Visitors from all over the world come to Kashi Vishwanath Temple to seek the blessings of Lord Shiva and to marvel at its beauty. The temple is a symbol of the deep spiritual and religious traditions of India and is an important pilgrimage site for Hindus




4. Mathura.

Mathura is a city located in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is considered the birthplace of Lord Krishna, one of the most revered gods in Hinduism. Mathura is famous for its ancient temples, including the Krishna Janmasthan Temple and the Dwarkadhish Temple. The city is also known for its colorful festivals, including Holi and Janmashtami, which celebrate the life and teachings of Lord Krishna. Visitors from all over the world come to Mathura to explore its rich cultural heritage and to seek the blessings of Lord Krishna. The city is a symbol of the deep spiritual and religious traditions of India and is an important pilgrimage site for Hindus.




5. Agra Fort.

Agra Fort is a historical monument located in the city of Agra, India. It was built in the 16th century by the Mughal emperor Akbar and is considered a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The fort is made of red sandstone and houses several palaces, mosques, and other buildings within its walls. One of the most famous structures within the fort is the Jahangir Mahal, which was built for the emperor's son. Another notable feature is the Diwan-i-Khas, a hall where the emperor held private audiences with important guests. Agra Fort is a must-see attraction for anyone visiting Agra and is an important part of India's rich history and culture.
