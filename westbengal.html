1. Howrah Bridge.

The Howrah Bridge is an iconic landmark located in the city of Kolkata, in the Indian state of West Bengal. It is one of the busiest and most famous bridges in India, connecting the twin cities of Kolkata and Howrah, and it is also a symbol of the city's rich history and culture. The bridge spans across the Hooghly River and is a stunning sight to behold, particularly during sunset and night-time when it is lit up in all its glory. The Howrah Bridge is not just a means of transportation but also a popular tourist attraction. Visitors can take a stroll along the bridge and enjoy the cool breeze and panoramic views of the river and the city skyline. The bridge is also a great spot for photography enthusiasts who can capture the stunning views of the city from different angles. The Howrah Bridge is an integral part of Kolkata's heritage and a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the city.


2. Victoria Memorial.

Victoria Memorial is an iconic landmark located in the heart of Kolkata, in the Indian state of West Bengal. It is a majestic white marble building that was built in memory of Queen Victoria, and it is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Kolkata. The Victoria Memorial is surrounded by sprawling gardens and fountains, and it is a perfect blend of Indian and British architecture. The monument houses a museum that showcases the history and heritage of India during the British colonial era, with an impressive collection of artifacts and paintings. Visitors can take a stroll through the galleries and admire the stunning exhibits, which offer a glimpse into India's rich history and cultural heritage. The Victoria Memorial is also a popular spot for photography enthusiasts, who can capture the stunning views of the monument and its surroundings. The monument is particularly beautiful during sunset and night-time when it is lit up in all its glory. The Victoria Memorial is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Kolkata, offering a glimpse into the city's rich history and culture.



3.Belur Math.

Belur Math is a spiritual and cultural center located in the Indian state of West Bengal, near Kolkata. It was established by Swami Vivekananda, a renowned Indian philosopher and spiritual leader, and it is the headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission. The campus of Belur Math is spread across several acres and is a beautiful blend of traditional Indian and modern architectural styles. The main temple of Belur Math is dedicated to Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, a famous saint and philosopher who lived in the 19th century, and it is a stunning example of the temple architecture of India. Visitors can also explore the museum at Belur Math, which showcases the life and teachings of Sri Ramakrishna and other spiritual leaders of India. The campus also houses a library, a bookshop, and several gardens and meditation halls, providing visitors with a serene and peaceful environment to connect with their spiritual side. Belur Math is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in spirituality and Indian culture, offering a unique and enriching experience that is sure to leave a lasting impression.




4.Birla Mandir.

Birla Mandir, also known as Lakshmi Narayan Temple, is a Hindu temple located in the city of Kolkata, in the Indian state of West Bengal. The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi, and it is a popular destination for tourists and devotees alike. The temple is known for its stunning architecture, which is a blend of traditional Hindu and modern styles, and is made entirely of white marble. The walls of the temple are adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures, depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. The main sanctum of the temple houses the idols of Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi, and visitors can offer prayers and seek blessings from the deities. The temple is surrounded by well-manicured gardens and fountains, providing visitors with a serene and peaceful environment to connect with their spiritual side. Birla Mandir is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Hinduism and Indian culture, offering a unique and enriching experience that is sure to leave a lasting impression.



5.Eden Gardens.

Eden Gardens is a cricket stadium located in the city of Kolkata, in the Indian state of West Bengal. It is one of the largest cricket stadiums in the world and is a popular destination for cricket enthusiasts and sports fans. The stadium has hosted numerous international cricket matches and has witnessed some of the most historic moments in the game of cricket. Apart from cricket, the stadium is also a popular venue for cultural and musical events, and it has played host to several renowned artists and performers from around the world. The stadium is surrounded by lush greenery and is a stunning sight to behold, particularly during night-time when it is lit up in all its glory. Visitors can take a guided tour of the stadium and learn about its history and heritage, or attend a cricket match and experience the thrill and excitement of the game. Eden Gardens is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in sports and entertainment, offering a unique and unforgettable experience that is sure to leave a lasting impression.
