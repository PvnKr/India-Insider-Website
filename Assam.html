1. Kaziranga National Park.

Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the state of Assam, India. It is known for its one-horned rhinoceros and is also home to many other animals such as elephants, tigers, and deer. The park is spread over an area of 430 square kilometers and is situated on the banks of the Brahmaputra River. Kaziranga National Park is also a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 400 species of birds found here. The park is open for visitors from November to April, and jeep safaris are available to explore the park. It is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.




2. Haflong.

Haflong is a small hill station located in the state of Assam, India. It is known for its picturesque landscapes and breathtaking views of the hills. The town is situated at an altitude of 680 meters above sea level and is surrounded by lush green forests. Haflong is a popular destination for trekking, camping, and adventure activities. The town is also home to many tea gardens and is known for its locally produced organic tea. Haflong is a perfect place to relax and rejuvenate in the lap of nature. 3. Sivasagae.

Sivasagar is a historical town located in the state of Assam, India. It was once the capital of the Ahom Kingdom and is known for its rich history and culture. The town is home to many ancient temples and monuments such as the Rang Ghar, Kareng Ghar, and Talatal Ghar. These structures are examples of the unique architecture of the Ahom dynasty. Sivasagar is also known for its scenic beauty and is situated on the banks of the Brahmaputra River. The town is a popular tourist destination and attracts visitors from all over the world. Sivasagar is a must-visit destination for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.






4. Jorhat.

Jorhat is a city located in the Indian state of Assam. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, tea gardens, and historical monuments. Jorhat is also known as the "Cultural Capital of Assam" because of its numerous cultural institutions like the Jorhat Institute of Science and Technology, Jorhat Engineering College, and the Assam Agricultural University. Jorhat has a vibrant economy based on agriculture, tea production, and tourism. The city is surrounded by tea gardens that produce high-quality Assam tea, which is famous all over the world. The city also has several historical monuments, including the Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary and the Majuli Island, which is the largest river island in the world. In summary, Jorhat is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage, a strong economy, and breathtaking natural beauty.




5. Majuli Island.

Majuli Island is the largest river island in the world, located in the Indian state of Assam. It is situated in the Brahmaputra River and is known for its natural beauty and cultural significance. The island is home to several indigenous tribes and is a hub of Assamese culture and tradition. Majuli Island is famous for its Vaishnavite monasteries called "Satras," which were established by the great Assamese saint Srimanta Sankardeva. These Satras are centers of performing arts, dance, and drama, and showcase the rich cultural heritage of Assam. Majuli Island is also a birdwatcher's paradise, and several migratory birds flock to the island during the winter months. The island offers a unique blend of culture and nature, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.
