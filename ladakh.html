1. Shanti Stupa.

Shanti Stupa is a magnificent white-domed Buddhist structure located on a hilltop in Leh, Ladakh. Built by a Japanese Buddhist organization in 1991, the stupa is a symbol of peace and prosperity, and it offers a panoramic view of the surrounding mountain ranges and the Leh city. The stupa is known for its impressive architecture, with intricate carvings and vibrant paintings depicting the life of Buddha. Visitors can climb a steep flight of stairs to reach the stupa, and once there, they can enjoy a serene and peaceful environment. The Shanti Stupa is a must-visit attraction for those seeking spiritual and cultural enlightenment, as well as for photography enthusiasts looking to capture the stunning beauty of Ladakh.


2. Tso Moriri.

Tso Moriri, also known as "Mountain Lake," is a breathtaking high-altitude lake located in the Changthang region of Ladakh. The lake is surrounded by snow-capped mountains and offers a serene and tranquil atmosphere. It is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts, photographers, and adventure seekers who come to explore the rugged terrain and wildlife. The crystal-clear waters of Tso Moriri are home to a variety of fish and birds, including the black-necked crane, which is a protected species. Visitors can indulge in activities such as camping, trekking, and bird watching. The best time to visit Tso Moriri is during the summer months from June to September when the weather is pleasant, and the lake is accessible. A trip to Tso Moriri is an opportunity to experience the raw and natural beauty of Ladakh and to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.



3.Thiksey Monastery.

Thiksey Monastery is a stunning Buddhist monastery located in the Leh district of Ladakh. Built in the 15th century, the monastery is one of the largest in Ladakh and is known for its impressive architecture, with its towering walls, prayer halls, and temples. The monastery is a center of spiritual and cultural significance and is home to a community of monks who follow the Gelugpa sect of Tibetan Buddhism. Visitors can explore the various chambers and halls, including the Maitreya Temple, which houses a beautiful statue of the future Buddha. The monastery also offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape, with the Indus River flowing nearby and the snow-capped Himalayan peaks in the distance. The Thiksey Monastery is a must-visit attraction for anyone seeking to explore the spiritual and cultural heritage of Ladakh.




4.Leh Palace.

Leh Palace is a majestic palace located in the heart of Leh town in Ladakh. It was built in the 17th century by King Sengge Namgyal and served as the royal residence for the Namgyal dynasty. The palace is an excellent example of Tibetan architecture, with its towering walls, large wooden balconies, and intricate carvings. Visitors can explore the various chambers and halls, including the royal apartments, the prayer room, and the museum. The museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that provide insight into the history and culture of Ladakh. From the palace, visitors can enjoy a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape, including the Leh town, the Indus River, and the snow-capped Himalayan peaks in the distance. The Leh Palace is a must-visit attraction for those interested in history, culture, and architecture, and it offers a glimpse into the grandeur and opulence of the Namgyal dynasty.



5.Zanskar.

Zanskar is a remote and rugged valley located in the Kargil district of Ladakh. The valley is surrounded by towering snow-capped mountains and is known for its spectacular landscapes, crystal-clear rivers, and ancient Buddhist monasteries. Visitors can indulge in a range of outdoor activities such as trekking, river rafting, and skiing, or they can explore the various cultural and historical sites such as the Karsha Monastery, the Zangla Fort, and the Stongdey Monastery. The valley is also home to the famous Chadar Trek, a winter trek that involves walking over the frozen Zanskar River, offering a unique and thrilling experience. The locals of Zanskar are friendly and hospitable, and visitors can immerse themselves in their vibrant culture and traditions. A trip to Zanskar is an opportunity to experience the raw and natural beauty of Ladakh and to escape the crowds and chaos of city life.
