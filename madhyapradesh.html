1. Bhimbetka rock shelters.

Bhimbetka Rock Shelters, located in Madhya Pradesh, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers a fascinating glimpse into the prehistoric era. The site consists of more than 750 rock shelters that showcase ancient cave paintings, some of which are estimated to be more than 30,000 years old. The paintings depict a range of subjects, including animals, human figures, and hunting scenes. The intricate and detailed paintings provide valuable insights into the lives and culture of the ancient people who once inhabited the region. Visitors can explore the site on foot, taking in the beautiful natural surroundings and the incredible historical artwork that adorns the rock faces. Bhimbetka Rock Shelters is a must-visit destination for history and culture enthusiasts, as well as anyone with an interest in the ancient civilizations that once thrived in this region of the world.


2. Gwalior Fort.

Gwalior Fort, located in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh, is a magnificent historical landmark that offers a glimpse into the rich and fascinating past of India. The fort was built in the 8th century and has been occupied by various dynasties over the centuries. Today, visitors can explore the fort's many structures, including palaces, temples, and other impressive buildings. One of the most remarkable features of the fort is the iconic Man Singh Palace, which boasts stunning architecture and intricate carvings. Visitors can also explore the beautiful Sas-Bahu Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is known for its elaborate carvings and intricate design. The fort offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for watching the sunset. Gwalior Fort is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Indian history and architecture, and it is sure to leave visitors awestruck by its grandeur and beauty.



3.Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga.

Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, located in the city of Ujjain in Madhya Pradesh, India, is one of the most sacred and revered shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India and is believed to be the only one where Lord Shiva is worshipped in his form of lingam, a symbol of his creative and regenerative powers. The temple complex is spread over a large area and comprises several smaller temples and shrines dedicated to various deities. The main temple is renowned for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings. The Mahakaleshwar temple is also home to a unique tradition of Bhasma Aarti, where the lingam is covered with sacred ashes and worshipped with lamps and incense. The temple draws devotees from all over the world who come to seek the blessings of Lord Shiva and experience the spiritual energy that permeates the air. Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Hindu mythology and spirituality, and it is sure to leave visitors with a sense of peace and serenity.




4.Sanchi Stupa.

Sanchi Stupa, located in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh, is a magnificent ancient Buddhist monument that dates back to the 3rd century BCE. It is one of the most important and well-preserved Buddhist sites in India, and is considered a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The site comprises several stupas, temples, monasteries and pillars that have been intricately carved with Buddhist teachings and symbolism. The main attraction of the site is the Great Stupa, which is believed to contain the relics of Lord Buddha. The stupa is surrounded by a fence and four gateways, known as Toranas, that are adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures. The site offers a unique opportunity to explore the rich history and culture of Buddhism in India, and visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the significance of each structure and its symbolism. Sanchi Stupa is a must-visit destination for history buffs, architecture enthusiasts and anyone with an interest in Buddhism, and it is sure to leave visitors awestruck by its grandeur and beauty.



5.Dhuandhar waterfall.

Dhuadhar Waterfall is a magnificent waterfall located in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh, in the city of Jabalpur. The waterfall is situated on the Narmada River, and it cascades down from a height of 98 feet, creating a spectacular misty spectacle that can be seen from far away. The name "Dhuadhar" means smoke cascade, and it refers to the thick mist that rises from the waterfall, giving it a mystical appearance. Visitors can enjoy a boat ride to get close to the waterfall and feel the cool mist on their faces. The waterfall is surrounded by beautiful lush greenery, and the nearby Marble Rocks create a stunning backdrop that adds to the beauty of the site. Dhuadhar Waterfall is a popular tourist destination, and visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as the Chausath Yogini Temple and the Balancing Rocks. The waterfall is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and anyone looking for a peaceful and refreshing break amidst stunning natural surroundings.
