1. Sela Pass.

Sela Pass is a high-altitude mountain pass situated in the Indian state of Arunachal Pradesh. It is located at an altitude of 13,700 feet above sea level and is known for its breathtaking views of the surrounding snow-capped mountains. The pass is a major tourist attraction and is a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts. It offers opportunities for trekking, camping, and sightseeing. The pass is also home to the Sela Lake, which is a major attraction for tourists visiting the area. Sela Pass is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the natural beauty and adventure of Arunachal Pradesh. Overall, Sela Pass is a true gem of the state and should not be missed by anyone visiting the region.




2. Bomdila.

Bomdila is a small town situated in the Indian state of Arunachal Pradesh. It is located at an altitude of 8,500 feet above sea level and is known for its scenic beauty and cultural richness. The town is famous for its apple orchards, which are a major source of livelihood for the local population. Bomdila is also home to the Bomdila Monastery, which is a major attraction for tourists visiting the town. The monastery is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful surroundings. Bomdila is a great destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in nature and spirituality. Overall, Bomdila is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the beauty and culture of Arunachal Pradesh.




3. Pasighat.

Pasighat is a town located in the state of Arunachal Pradesh in Northeast India. It is situated on the banks of the Siang River and is the headquarters of the East Siang district. Pasighat is a place of great cultural, historical, and natural significance. The town is surrounded by lush green forests, hills, and mountains and is home to diverse flora and fauna. Pasighat is known for its rich cultural heritage and is home to various tribes like Adi, Galo, Minyong, and others. It is also a popular destination for adventure activities like trekking, rafting, and angling.




4. Ziro.

Ziro is a picturesque valley town located in the Lower Subansiri district of Arunachal Pradesh. It is known for its scenic beauty, unique festivals, and rich tribal culture. Ziro is home to the Apatani tribe, who are known for their unique way of life, including their traditional farming techniques, unique tattoo culture, and love for music and dance. The town is surrounded by lush green forests and is home to beautiful rice fields and ancient stone structures. Ziro is also a popular destination for adventure activities like trekking and camping, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.




5. Dirang.

Dirang is a small hill station located in the West Kameng district of Arunachal Pradesh. It is known for its scenic beauty, hot springs, and ancient monasteries. The town is surrounded by snow-capped mountains, dense forests, and sparkling rivers, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. Dirang is home to the Monpa tribe, who are known for their unique culture, hospitality, and handicrafts. The town is also a popular destination for adventure activities like trekking and camping. The hot springs in Dirang are believed to have therapeutic properties and are a major attraction for tourists visiting the town. Overall, Dirang is a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and adventure.
