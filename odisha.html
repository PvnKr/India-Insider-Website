1. Konark Sun Temple.

Konark Sun Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the state of Odisha in eastern India. The temple is dedicated to the Hindu sun god Surya and is one of the most stunning examples of ancient Indian temple architecture. The temple was built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty and is known for its intricate carvings and impressive engineering. The temple is shaped like a chariot and is pulled by seven stone horses, each representing a day of the week. The temple is also known for its erotic carvings, which are considered to be some of the finest examples of Indian erotic art. The temple was partially destroyed in the 18th century, but many of the impressive carvings and sculptures still remain. Konark Sun Temple is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in ancient Indian architecture and history, and it is one of the most popular tourist attractions in eastern India.


2. Shree Jagannath Temple Puri.

Shree Jagannath Temple in Puri is a famous Hindu temple located in the coastal city of Puri, in the state of Odisha. The temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, and is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus. The temple is famous for its annual Rath Yatra or the Chariot festival, where the deities of Lord Jagannath, Lord Balabhadra, and Goddess Subhadra are taken out in a grand procession on chariots. The temple's architecture is stunning, with intricate carvings and artwork on the walls and pillars. The temple complex also includes smaller shrines dedicated to various deities and a large kitchen that serves thousands of devotees every day. The temple is known for its unique tradition of offering Mahaprasad or cooked food as a form of divine blessings, which is considered to be highly auspicious. Shree Jagannath Temple is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Hindu culture and spirituality, and it attracts thousands of devotees and tourists every year.



3.Lingraj Temple.

Lingraj Temple is an ancient Hindu temple located in Bhubaneswar, the capital city of the eastern Indian state of Odisha. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the most revered temples in India. The temple's architecture is a stunning example of the Kalinga style of temple architecture, characterized by its towering spire, intricately carved stone walls, and numerous small shrines within the temple complex. The temple is believed to have been built in the 11th century and has been a major pilgrimage site for Shaivites, devotees of Lord Shiva, for centuries. The temple is known for its elaborate rituals and traditions, including the daily offerings of flowers, fruits, and other items to the deity. Lingraj Temple is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Hindu culture and spirituality and is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Bhubaneswar. The temple's grandeur and spiritual atmosphere make it a truly unique and unforgettable experience for visitors.




4.nandankanan zoological park.

Nandankanan Zoological Park is a popular wildlife sanctuary located in Bhubaneswar, the capital city of the Indian state of Odisha. The park is spread over 400 hectares of lush green forest and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. The park is known for its conservation efforts and breeding programs for endangered species like the Indian pangolin, white tiger, and Asiatic lion. Visitors to the park can take a guided tour of the park and see animals like tigers, lions, elephants, deer, and monkeys up close. The park also has a botanical garden, aquarium, and reptile park, making it a fun and educational destination for families and wildlife enthusiasts. One of the highlights of the park is the white tiger safari, where visitors can see rare white tigers in their natural habitat. Nandankanan Zoological Park is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in wildlife conservation and offers a unique opportunity to see some of India's most magnificent animals in a beautiful and natural setting.



5.Chilika Lake.

Chilika Lake is a large lagoon located in the eastern Indian state of Odisha. Spread over an area of 1,100 square kilometers, it is the largest coastal lagoon in India and the second-largest lagoon in the world. The lake is separated from the Bay of Bengal by a long, narrow sandbar and is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna. The lake is a bird watcher's paradise and is home to over 160 species of birds, including migratory birds that visit the lake during the winter months. Visitors to Chilika Lake can take a boat ride through the lake and explore its many islands and wetlands. The lake is also known for its fishery resources and is an important source of livelihood for the local fishing communities. The lake is surrounded by picturesque villages and offers visitors a glimpse into the unique culture and way of life of the local people. Chilika Lake is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in nature, wildlife, and culture and offers a truly unforgettable experience of India's natural beauty.
