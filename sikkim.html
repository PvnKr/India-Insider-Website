1. Gangtok.

Gangtok is the capital city of the Indian state of Sikkim, situated in the Eastern Himalayas. It is a popular tourist destination, known for its natural beauty, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. The city is surrounded by mountains and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan range. Visitors can explore the various monasteries and temples in the city, such as the Rumtek Monastery, Enchey Monastery, and Hanuman Tok Temple, which offer a glimpse into the spiritual and cultural traditions of Sikkim. The city is also home to several parks and gardens, such as the Ridge Park and Flower Exhibition Centre, which showcase the diverse flora and fauna of the region. The city is known for its vibrant street markets, where visitors can shop for traditional handicrafts, souvenirs, and local delicacies. Gangtok is also a popular destination for adventure sports such as trekking, mountaineering, and river rafting, offering visitors an adrenaline-pumping experience amidst the stunning natural surroundings. Gangtok is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and seeking a peaceful and rejuvenating experience amidst nature.


2. Khangchendzonga National Park.

Khangchendzonga National Park is a protected area located in the Indian state of Sikkim, in the Eastern Himalayas. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the most ecologically diverse regions in the world, home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. The park is named after Mount Khangchendzonga, which is the third-highest mountain peak in the world and a sacred site for the indigenous people of Sikkim. The park offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan range, including several snow-capped peaks and glaciers. Visitors can explore the park on foot or on horseback, and can spot several endangered species such as the red panda, snow leopard, and Himalayan black bear. The park is also home to several high-altitude lakes, such as the Khecheopalri Lake and the Gurudongmar Lake, which are considered sacred by the locals and offer a serene and peaceful environment for visitors to connect with nature. The park is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in wildlife and nature, offering a unique and unforgettable experience amidst the stunning natural surroundings of the Eastern Himalayas.



3.Pelling.

Pelling is a small town located in the Indian state of Sikkim, situated in the foothills of the Himalayas. It is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning natural beauty, panoramic views, and serene environment. The town offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan range, including the snow-capped peaks of Mount Khangchendzonga. Visitors can explore the various monasteries and temples in the town, such as the Pemayangtse Monastery and the Sangachoeling Monastery, which offer a glimpse into the spiritual and cultural traditions of Sikkim. The town is also home to several natural attractions such as the Khecheopalri Lake and the Kanchenjunga Falls, which offer a serene and peaceful environment for visitors to connect with nature. Pelling is a popular destination for trekking and hiking, offering several scenic trails and routes for visitors to explore the surrounding landscapes. The town is also known for its warm and welcoming locals, who offer a unique insight into the local traditions and culture of Sikkim. Pelling is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and seeking a peaceful and rejuvenating experience amidst nature.




4.Tsomgo cho.

Tsomgo Lake, also known as Tsongmo Lake or Changu Lake, is a glacial lake located in the Indian state of Sikkim, approximately 40 kilometers from the capital city of Gangtok. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the state and is known for its pristine natural beauty and tranquil environment. The lake is situated at an altitude of 12,310 feet above sea level and is surrounded by snow-capped mountains and lush greenery, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the area around the lake on foot or on horseback, and can witness the beauty of the changing colors of the lake throughout the day. The lake is also home to several species of migratory birds, adding to its natural charm. The area around the lake is also known for its traditional Tibetan culture, with several local shops selling traditional handicrafts and souvenirs. The lake is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Sikkim, offering a unique and unforgettable experience amidst the stunning natural surroundings of the Eastern Himalayas.



5. Nathula.

Nathula Pass is a mountain pass located in the Indian state of Sikkim, at an altitude of 14,140 feet above sea level. It is situated on the Indo-China border and is one of the highest motorable passes in the world. The pass was once an important trade route between India and Tibet and is now a popular tourist destination, offering stunning views of the Himalayan range and an insight into the local culture and traditions of Sikkim. Visitors need a permit to visit Nathula Pass, which can be obtained from the Sikkim Tourism Office in Gangtok. The pass is open for visitors only during specific months of the year, typically from April to November, due to harsh weather conditions during the winter months. Visitors can explore the area around the pass on foot, taking in the stunning natural beauty and visiting the various monuments and memorials located in the area. Nathula Pass is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the breathtaking beauty of the Eastern Himalayas and the rich cultural heritage of Sikkim.
