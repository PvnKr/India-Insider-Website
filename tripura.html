1. Unakoti.

Unakoti is an archaeological site located in the northern part of Tripura, India. It is famous for its rock-cut sculptures and carvings of Hindu deities, including Lord Shiva. The name Unakoti means "one less than a crore" in Bengali, referring to the legend that there are one less than ten million sculptures at the site. The site is believed to be over 1000 years old and is a popular tourist destination in Tripura. The sculptures and carvings are spread over a hill and the surrounding forests, creating a unique and picturesque landscape. Unakoti is an important cultural and historical site in Tripura and is worth visiting to learn about the region's rich heritage..




2. Tripura Sundari Temple.

Tripura Sundari Temple is an ancient temple located in the town of Udaipur, Tripura, India. It is dedicated to the goddess Tripura Sundari, who is believed to be an incarnation of goddess Parvati. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings, which are typical of the traditional temple architecture of Northeast India. The temple complex includes several smaller shrines and a large pond, which adds to the overall charm of the place. The temple is an important religious and cultural site in Tripura and attracts a large number of visitors every year. It is a great place to visit to learn about the region's rich heritage and to experience the traditional rituals and practices associated with the temple.




3. Neermahal.

Neermahal is a beautiful palace located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake in the Indian state of Tripura. It was built in the early 20th century by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya, who was the then ruler of the state. The palace is a fine example of a fusion of Indian and Western architectural styles. Neermahal served as the summer residence of the royal family and was also used for conducting cultural and religious events. It has a unique waterway system that connects it to the mainland, and the palace is surrounded by lush greenery and pristine water. Today, Neermahal is a popular tourist attraction and is managed by the Tripura Tourism Development Corporation. Visitors can take a boat ride to the palace and explore its grandeur and history.




4. Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary.

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary is a popular tourist destination located in Tripura, India. The sanctuary covers an area of 18.53 square kilometers and is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The sanctuary was established in 1987 and is known for its rich biodiversity. It has a large population of primates, including the spectacled langur, capped langur, and rhesus macaque. Sepahijala is also home to many species of birds, including the Oriental pied hornbill and the Indian pitta. Visitors to the sanctuary can explore its various trails, go birdwatching, and take a boat ride on the lake. The sanctuary also has a zoo that houses many rare and endangered species, including clouded leopards and red pandas. Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-visit for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts who want to experience the beauty and diversity of Tripura's natural landscape.




5. Ujjayanta Palace

Ujjayanta Palace is a magnificent palace located in the heart of Agartala, the capital city of Tripura in India. The palace was constructed in the early 20th century during the reign of Maharaja Radha Kishore Manikya and was completed in 1901. The palace boasts a beautiful fusion of Mughal and European architectural styles, and its grandeur is reflected in its beautiful gardens, courtyards, and fountains. The palace was converted into a museum in 2013 and now houses a collection of rare artifacts, paintings, and sculptures, as well as exhibits on Tripura's rich cultural heritage. Ujjayanta Palace is a must-visit for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts who want to explore the grandeur and beauty of Tripura's rich past.
