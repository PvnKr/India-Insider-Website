1. Umiam Lake.

Umiam Lake is a picturesque man-made reservoir located in the Indian state of Meghalaya, approximately 15 kilometers north of the capital city of Shillong. The lake was created by damming the Umiam River and is surrounded by lush green hills and forests, making it a popular tourist destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities such as boating, kayaking, fishing, and zip-lining, or simply take in the scenic beauty of the lake and the surrounding landscape. The area around Umiam Lake is also home to several local villages, allowing visitors to experience the local culture and traditions of the Khasi tribe. There are several resorts and guesthouses located near the lake, offering comfortable accommodation and a range of amenities. The best time to visit Umiam Lake is from October to April when the weather is pleasant and the lake is at its most picturesque. A visit to Umiam Lake is a must for anyone traveling to Meghalaya, offering a peaceful and serene retreat in the midst of nature's bounty.


2. Kyllang Rock.

Kyllang Rock is a massive granite rock located in the Indian state of Meghalaya, approximately 10 kilometers from the town of Jowai. It is considered to be one of the largest natural rock formations in Asia, standing at a height of around 5400 feet above sea level. The rock is believed to have been formed over 20 million years ago and is an important site for geologists and rock climbers alike. Visitors can explore the rock on foot, taking in the stunning natural beauty of the surrounding landscape and admiring the unique rock formations that have been sculpted over millions of years. The area around Kyllang Rock is also home to several local villages, allowing visitors to experience the local culture and traditions of the Khasi tribe. The best time to visit Kyllang Rock is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and the rock is at its most picturesque. A visit to Kyllang Rock is a must for anyone traveling to Meghalaya, offering a glimpse into the ancient geological history of the region and the breathtaking beauty of its natural landscape.



3.Cherrapunji.

Cherrapunji, also known as Sohra, is a town located in the Indian state of Meghalaya, known for being one of the wettest places on earth. The town receives heavy rainfall throughout the year, making it lush and green, with cascading waterfalls and dense forests. Visitors can explore the natural beauty of the region by trekking through the lush green hills, visiting the stunning waterfalls such as Nohkalikai Falls, Dainthlen Falls, and Wei Sawdong Falls, and taking in the breathtaking views from the many viewpoints in the area. Cherrapunji is also known for its unique living root bridges, which are formed by weaving the roots of the Ficus elastica tree across streams, creating a natural bridge that can support the weight of several people. The town is also home to several cultural sites, including the Nohsngithiang Falls, the Khasi Heritage Village, and the Ramakrishna Mission, offering visitors a glimpse into the local culture and traditions. The best time to visit Cherrapunji is from October to May, when the weather is pleasant and the waterfalls are at their fullest. A visit to Cherrapunji is a must for anyone traveling to Meghalaya, offering a unique and unforgettable experience of nature's raw beauty.




4.Shillong.

Shillong is a picturesque hill station located in the Indian state of Meghalaya. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and pleasant weather, making it a popular tourist destination. The town is surrounded by lush green hills and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the town by taking a stroll through the bustling markets, enjoying the local cuisine, and visiting cultural sites such as the Don Bosco Centre for Indigenous Cultures, the Cathedral of Mary Help of Christians, and the Shillong Peak, which offers panoramic views of the town. Shillong is also known for its vibrant music scene and is often referred to as the "rock capital of India." The town is home to several music festivals throughout the year, including the Shillong Autumn Festival and the Shillong International Music Festival. The best time to visit Shillong is from October to May, when the weather is pleasant and the town is at its most beautiful. A visit to Shillong is a must for anyone traveling to Meghalaya, offering a unique and unforgettable experience of nature, culture, and music.



5.Laitlum Canyon.

Laitlum Canyon is a scenic gorge located in the Indian state of Meghalaya. It is a hidden gem that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is becoming increasingly popular among tourists seeking an offbeat experience. The word "Laitlum" translates to "end of hills," and true to its name, the canyon is located at the end of a winding road that takes visitors through the beautiful hills of Meghalaya. Visitors can explore the canyon by taking a leisurely hike through the lush green forests, enjoying the cool breeze and panoramic views of the hills and valleys. The canyon is also a great spot for photography, offering numerous vantage points for capturing stunning photos of the landscape. The best time to visit Laitlum Canyon is from October to May, when the weather is pleasant and the landscape is at its most beautiful. A visit to Laitlum Canyon is a must for anyone traveling to Meghalaya, offering a unique and unforgettable experience of nature's raw beauty.
